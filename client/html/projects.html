Student creations

Systems Design students and graduates are an innovative and entrepreneurial bunch, and over the years have created many successful companies and projects.

Companies

Miovision
Kurtis McBride, Tony Brijpaul, Class of 2004

The company started by three University of Waterloo friends in a basement has evolved into the leading authority on traffic data. Miovision began as a traffic solution company, but their continued approach to innovation and dedication to solving real-world problems has led them to where they stand today: helping cities achieve their smart city visions.

NexJ Systems, Janna Systems
William Tatham, Class of 1983

NexJ Systems Inc. is a leading provider of enterprise customer relationship management (CRM) solutions. Prior to establishing NexJ Systems in 2003, William was the Founder, Chairman and CEO of Janna Systems, which created enterprise CRM software for financial services. William received an honorary doctorate from the University of Waterloo in 2012, and is the namesake of the Tatham Center on campus.

Desire2Learn
John Baker, Class of 2000

John founded D2L in 1999, at the age of twenty-two, while attending the University of Waterloo as a Systems Design Engineering student. He saw that online digital tools could open the door to entirely new and more effective models, and create learning experiences that are more personal, engaging and inspiring. The company is on the journey of transforming the way the world learns.

Vidyard
Michael Litt, Devon Galloway, Class of 2010

Vidyard was the first video production company to offer video hosting, analytics, and performance guarantees. Started as a Capstone Design project, the company soon graduated from the Y Combinator program and focuses exclusively on video marketing technology.

Pebble
Eric Migicovsky, Class of 2009

Eric developed the first commercially successful smartwatch, the Pebble Smartwatch. Before shutting down in 2016, Pebble graduated from Y Combinator and sold over a million devices. Eric describes how SYDE helped his growing entrepreneurial career: "100% help. Best undergrad engineering program for people who like to work on projects (ie design and build things). A++ would attend again. Also, the coop program at UWaterloo really helps you see what working at 'real' companies is like. My one regret is not doing more coop terms at startups."

Nulogy Corporation
Sean Kirby, Jason Tham, Kevin Wong, Jason Yuen, Class of 2002

"Nulogy began with a desire to be a special kind of company. We wanted to solve really hard, unsolved problems. We wanted to be laser-focused on delivering concrete customer value right out of the gates. Most importantly, we wanted to be a company that was meaningful." Nulogy helps brands deliver customized, traceable, and safer products to consumers with greater speed and value with less waste.

82 Labs Inc: Morning Recovery
Sisun Lee, Class of 2014

In just three years Sisun has worked at Facebook, Uber, Tesla, and has now created his own startup to cure hangovers. Morning Recovery made $1 million in their first three months.

HelpWear
Andre Bertram, Class of 2021

Andre and his co-founder are developing wearable products, like the HeartWatch, a 24/7 wearable heart monitoring system that will contact EMS in the event of a heart attack. The company has not only graduated from Y Combinator, but was showcased on 'Dragon's Den' and recieved an investment from every Dragon.

Sodatone
Jerry Zhang, Class of 2019

Jerry Zhang has a long entrepreneurial track record, the latest of which is Sodatone. Recently acquired by Warner Music, this machine learning start-up helps record labels to find and track the next hit artist.
