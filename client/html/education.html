What do we learn?

Systems is how we see the world, design is how we change it.

Unofficial Systems Design motto

Systems Design Engineering (SYDE) has a focus on systems thinking and design thinking. Systems thinking, along with mathematical skills, is used to model complex physical problems. Design thinking is applied to create human-centered solutions to these problems. We tackle interdisciplinary problems that cover many areas of engineering, including software, hardware, mechanical, and industrial.

SYDE is an accredited engineering program, meaning its graduates are eligible for the Professional Engineering designation through PEO. As such we learn a lot of theory in math and physics, as well as applications in hardware and software. The first two years are about understanding the physical world, and acquiring analytic and math skills. This toolbox of skills allows us to solve real, hands-on problems involving large systems.

Design is a complementary skill set of processes for planning and executing solutions to problems. The perfect complement to engineering, design is rarely taught outright to engineers. Human-centered design processes allow an engineer to think beyond the technical aspects of a problem. Design ensures that an engineer's solution "fits human desires, needs, and capabilities. After all, why do we make products? We make them for people to use." We practice our design skills every term through project courses.

The skills that we learn in SYDE can be applied to any problem in any field. Our core courses teach us a diverse mix of applied and technical skills, broken down by field below. Students also have access to a wide array of technical electives, and can specialize in nearly any other field of engineering.

Design

Design is really an act of communication, which means having a deep understanding of the person with whom the designer is communicating.

Donald A. Norman, The Design of Everyday Things

What sets Systems apart from your typical engineering program is our emphasis on designing for all people. Design is more than making things aesthetically pleasing; it is a method to design useable solutions. Human Centered Design means questioning the problem that you think you need to solve. It involves deeply understanding the people who will use your engineered solution. Design is all about starting with people, instead of starting technical. This process is an effective way to solve engineering problems, as well as the human concerns that accompany them.

The design toolbox

Design thinking is a human-centered approach to innovation that draws from the designer’s toolkit to integrate the needs of people, the possibilities of technology, and the requirements for business success.

 Tim Brown, President and CEO of IDEO

While many of our design courses are project-focused, there is a great set of tools that we learn to apply.

  • Human Centered Design: Used to understand the perspectives of real users, and turn an idea into a prototype
  • Project Management: Take into consideration schedules, stakeholders, and budgets.
  • Human Factors Engineering: Human Factors Engineering: Consider the mental and physical abilities and limitations of users.
  • Impact on Society: Engineered solutions exist within and impact many facets of society.
  • Design Methods: Apply industry methods such as Google Ventures' Design Sprint process

Applying design thinking

Systems

I have yet to see any problem, however complicated, which, when looked at in the right way, did not become still more complicated.

 Poul Anderson

Engineering problems are complicated, and they will only grow in complexity as society progresses. Take something as seemingly simple as an iPhone. Look inside and you will find:

  • A battery worked on by chemical engineers.
  • Circuit boards worked on by computer engineers.
  • A radio/cell system worked on by electrical engineers.
  • A screen with programs worked on by software engineers.
  • A manufacturing process worked on by industrial engineers.

These small parts connected together as an iPhone create an impact on society greater than they could on their own. This is a system, and one that a Systems Design Engineer may work on.

Systems thinking

Systems are an organized assembly of interconnected components. The interactions of components create behaviours that affect the system as a whole. This definition is general on purpose; systems can be anything. The process you learn for analyzing the drive-train of a car can be applied to analyzing the resilience of an entire city. Systems thinking is a method for designing and managing these diverse systems. In essence it is a unique way of viewing problems.

Modelling the world

As engineers, use systems thinking to create models of physical systems so as to understand engineering problems. This is why we take so many physics and math courses, so that we can apply our skills to modelling dynamic systems. Such problems may be as simple as a slinky that continues bouncing even after you drop it down a staircase. This also applies to complex problems involving many moving parts, stresses, and applied forces.
