co-op

Show me the money

Waterloo’s co-op program is absolutely fantastic. Why? You get experience in the fields that you are interested in, not only to gain contacts for your future career, but also to discover what you like (and don’t like) to do. Your six co-op positions are paid, and will also help you fund an education that grows more costly every year.

the co-op process

What is 4-stream?

Waterloo has different academic/co-op term patterns, with SYDE being solely 4-Stream. This mean students start their first work term after 4-months of school. This means getting job experience sooner, and ending your degree off with 2 consecutive academic terms.

For future students: While the idea of working so soon may be scary, it does have some advantages. You will have less competition for jobs in the winter, and will start earning money sooner than your peers in 8-stream!

What are applications like?

When you first start 1A, you will have a few weeks to prepare your resume (start in the summer, and get upper years to review it). Then, you will have a week long job application period, where you can browse the job postings on Waterloo Works and submit your application. The number of job postings number in the thousands, but don’t be too picky at first because a job is not guaranteed.

After applications, employers will slowly send out interview bookings, during which time you can choose an interview timeslot. Most interviews are held in person, on campus, which is very convenient for students. After interviews, both students and employers will submit job rankings, and then students are matched (hopefully) with their jobs for the next term. If you don’t get matched, you have the opportunity to keep applying so don’t ever lose hope.

finances

How much do students earn?

Your first stop for checking average earnings should be the official CECA webpage. Since employers are subsidized by the government, wages are quite reasonable. Be warned though, wages on the CECA page are skewed downward as they exclude most USA-based jobs which tend to have higher pay and include food/housing. A fair number of employers base their wages on this data, so it is still a good representation. Can I pay for my education?

While there is no guarantee that co-op can pay for your entire education, it certainly can take a load off of students who would have had to take out large loans to pay for their education. Of the Class of 2017, almost half the class graduated with no student debt, and the mean amount of debt was less that $10,000. With the cost of tuition and living expenses for one term being just over $10,000, this paints a bright picture for future SYDE students. (See pg 73–74 of 2017ClassProfile).
