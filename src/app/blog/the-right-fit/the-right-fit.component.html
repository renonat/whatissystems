Lilo (and Stitch)

There’s no denying it, engineering is tough. But, the wonderful SYDE community certainly makes our time at Waterloo worth it. Our professors care about us as a cohort and as individuals, and support us in our endeavours. Upper years are a stones throw away, and can be bumped into in the hallways or in our SYDE student lounge. There is a connection between cohorts, with shared events and traditions that bring us all closer together throughout the years.

The SYDE community is centred around the approx. 100 students who make up your cohort, many of whom you will spend 5 years with. They will be your support system through all 36 core courses that you take, and many of your electives. This means that when a tough assignment rolls through, you have friends to tackle it with. When you spend so much of your day with one group of people, how could you not find friends for life?

Social engineering

SYDE is known as the most social engineering program here at Waterloo, and we take that role seriously. Besides spending so much time together within our cohort, we also have many opportunities to get to know our upper years. There are times for professional growth such as resume critiques and coffee chats with the soon to be graduates. There are also more social traditions such as All Systems Go (and it’s followup All Systems Classy), which are held every term in the residence of an upper year student. You can come out to our Coffeehouse. Mid-term you can come out to our Coffeehouse, a great community gathering with performances by students and professors! Add into the mix holiday celebrations, spirit weeks, and nights out in Uptown Waterloo, and you have a experience you will never forget!

Student spaces

Our SYDE home is on the top floor of Engineering 5 (E5), which we share with Biomed. Here we have our primary classrooms, as well as all of our student spaces. You will find a student lounge accessible by all SYDE and students, as well as a workshop and computer lab for working on design projects.

Show me the data

We can talk all we want about the subjective characteristics of SYDE, but we also have the data to prove it (from the Class of 2017 SYDE profile):

47% of the class would invite 10 or more people from the class to their birthday party.
No student reported that they had 0 friends in the class. Yay!
More than half the class said that at least 4 of their 5 closest friends made during university were SYDE classmates.
Number of terms
42% of the class went to a SYDE event (ASG, ASC, Coffeehouse) in all 8 academic terms, and two thirds of the class went in 6 or more terms
