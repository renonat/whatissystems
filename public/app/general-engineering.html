Is this general engineering?

People have trouble describing SYDE because it does not fit into a stereotype of what engineering work should be. But, this does not discredit the work that we do. While Systems Design may appear very broad, it is NOT a general engineering program that you might find at other Universities. We learn the specific skills of design and systems thinking. One than can apply these skills to their field of interest.

There is a trade-off that comes with being a SYDE student: deeply specializing in another field may need extracurricular work. For example, you will need to put in extra time to practice and learn to be as specialized as a mechanical engineer. Thus if you are highly passionate about a single field, consider directly pursuing that field. As a SYDE student, you must be proactive to find your niche. But, the knowledge you gain from SYDE will give you great freedom within your career.

Can I specialize?

While you can specialize through co-op terms and side projects, you can also take specialized electives. These electives allow you to become an expert at what YOU choose to focus on. There will be a great deal of freedom to learn in your field of choice, but it does need some thinking about disciplines beforehand. If you take a variety of diverse electives, there is a risk that you will end up as the “jack of all trades but master of none.” Give consideration to your elective choices, as they form the skillset that you take to the workforce as a practicing engineer.

If you do not know where your passions lie for your career, that is okay. Many spend their first two years exploring different fields through co-op positions. By midway through your degree, you should have an idea of your career goals and spend your time honing your skills. While it is okay to explore a variety of fields, be aware that it may impact the ease of finding co-op positions.

What are my choices?

While you can choose technical electives at your discretion, the department and faculty have made packages of electives that you can choose from to focus your studies.

Systems Design Technical Elective Packages
Created by the SYDE department. Do not provide any designation on your transcript.

Human Systems Engineering
Develop skills applicable to the design and analysis of systems that interact closely with humans.
Intelligent Systems
Develop software systems with artificial perception and reasoning, think machine learning and A.I.
Societal and Environmental Systems
Study societal and environmental systems, and the interactions between the natural and social environment.
Systems Modelling and Analysis
Learn the mathematics and methods needed to understand and integrate complex systems.
Computer Engineering
Augment SYDE curriculum with a strong background in the hardware and software of computer systems.

Designated Options
Provide a designation on your transcript. May require overloading your schedule to complete all required courses.

Biomechanics
Take the engineer's role in solving health care problems.
Entrepreneurship
Provides academic and experiential learning with respect to venture creation and corporate entrepreneurship.
Environmental Engineering
Emphasises environmental concerns, impact assessment, and solving/managing environmental problems.
International Studies in Engineering
Enriches courses on the global nature of engineering with international experience.
Life Sciences
Increases understanding of the structure and function of biological systems.
Management Sciences
Prepares students for decision making roles in business and technology management.
Mathematics
Provides a broad background in either pure or applied mathematics.
Mechatronics
Examines systems that link and integrate mechanical, electrical, and computer engineering.
Physical Sciences
Provides deeper knowledge of physics, chemistry, and Earth & Environmental Sciences.
Statistics
Acquire a background in applied statistics, relevant for quality control and experimental design.
Water Resources
Related to the development, management, and protection of water resources.
